Paper presentations

For the second assessment, you will choose a scientific paper from the list below, read it, understand it, and present it individually to the CO663 team.

The presentation/viva will last 15 minutes and and we except you to speak for 10 minutes and keep 5 minutes for questions/discussions.

The main objectives of this assignment is to gain some experience in reading scientific papers on programming languages and learn new cutting edge and/or influential results in the area that have had an impact on today's programming languages.

Guidelines

We don't expect you to understand every single technical details in the paper you have chosen. A good presentation would show that you have understood the "big picture" (e.g., what is the main idea the authors are presenting? what problems are they trying to solve?) and that you have understood some of the technicalities (in particular regarding the semantics and type systems for the language presented).

Feel free to post any questions you might have on piazza. If needed, we will dedicate time during classes and lectures for discussing answers to these questions.

For this assessment, you can safely ignore the proofs and algorithmic aspects of these papers.

The morning paper has a good blog post on how to read a programming language paper. The blog contains also lots of examples of good paper summaries. You can use this sort of summaries as a template for yours.
