Paper presentations

For the second assessment, you will choose a scientific paper from the list below, read it, and understand it. You will write a short (blog-post-style) document (max 1500 words) that gives a good overview of the paper (in groups of 2 to 4). See the morning paper for a good example.

The main objectives of this assignment is to gain some experience in reading scientific papers on programming languages and learn new cutting edge and/or influential results in the area that have had an impact on today's programming languages

Any question ==> ask on Piazza.

Guidelines

We don't expect you to understand every single technical details in the paper you have chosen. A good presentation would show that you have understood the "big picture" (e.g., what is the main idea the authors are presenting? what problems are they trying to solve?) and that you have understood some of the technicalities (in particular regarding the semantics and type systems for the language presented).

Feel free to post any questions you might have on piazza. If needed, we will dedicate time during classes and lectures for discussing answers to these questions.

For this assessment, you can safely ignore the proofs and algorithmic aspects of these papers.

The morning paper has a good blog post on how to read a programming language paper. The blog contains also lots of examples of good paper summaries. You can use this sort of summaries as a template for your presentations.

See also What is PL research and how is it useful? on the Programming Languages Enthusiast.

Evaluation criteria

  • Understanding: does the presentation demonstrate a good understanding of the paper?
  • Coverage: does the presentation give a complete overview of the source material?
  • Evaluation: does the presentation demonstrate a critical evaluation of the paper?
  • Clarity: is the presentation clear/accessible to the audience?
  • PL theory: does the presentation make clear links with the lectures?
  • Group work: are all the members of the group participating in the presentation?
